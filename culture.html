Bubble tea is a Taiwanese tea-based drink, also known as boba, pearl milk tea, tapioca milk tea, boba tea, or boba nai cha. It originated from Taiwan and was brought to the United States by immigrants during the 1990â€™s. Since then, this drink has gained popularity and spread to many states and nations.

Bubble tea was rumored to be created by the product development manager, Ms. Lin Hsiu Hui, of a teahouse, Cun Shui Tang, because she was bored. She dropped fen yuan (sweetened tapioca pudding) into her Assam iced tea and the rest was history. This bubble tea invention became a new sensation and soon everyone was selling it.
